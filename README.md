Dr. Christopher Williams Issues Statement on Interpreting Results from applying Critical Race Framework Study

October 29, 2025

I conducted the Critical Race Framework study within the resource and time constraints of doctoral research, including approval requirements to meet institutional deadlines for timely graduation. A delimitation of that writing effort was the under-exploration of interpreting results when users apply the Critical Race Framework rubric to research studies.  My critical appraisal tool was the first of its kind concerning the scientific use of race in public health research. It gives any faculty member or student twenty areas of potential weaknesses for evaluating research quality. There were also heightened political considerations within doctoral research that I had to consider.

Yet, full interpretation is not a simple matter of the results from scoring, which is up to 20 scores of "high quality". This essay is intended to more fully guide users. It addresses interpretation - the stage after an article has been scored. My purpose is to avoid misinterpretation or potential misuse of the framework.

Checklist is First Stage of Critical Appraisal - The Critical Race Framework study provided an extensive review of the literature in statistical theory, measurement theory, and psychometric analysis. The Conceptual Framework for Critical Appraisal indicated a key consideration for interpretation. Namely, full critical appraisal in the context of the CRF should not be considered cumulative, which a summation score might suggest. I did not address this issue adequately in the study. Assessing overall study quality on the total score is incomplete. Rather, each iterative step to determine overall study quality should be based on research quality from the prior steps. I discussed this more fully in several chapters of the study. 

The House of Cards - If a study has high reliability, and strong validity, then one can then proceed to evaluate claims of causal inference and external validity.  If a study fails ("no or low reliability") at the first stage of reliability evaluation, it renders all subsequent analysis and interpretations highly skeptical and indeterminable. If a study cannot consistently and reliably obtain the same measure, then the rest of the study falls like a house of cards, particularly where that measure comprises a major variable of interest - independent or dependent variable. Validity cannot be established without reliability. Data interpretation cannot be well-founded without acceptable validity. Data results, thus, become unreliable and uninterpretable. Similar to what we are seeing in research using racial variables, a study could have the most sophisticated of statistical analyses, but if it has little to no reliability, there is a fatal blow to study quality.

Flowchart for Ending Critical Appraisal or Data Interpretation When Race is a Primary Variable of Interest
Thus, we arrive at a more accurate picture of critical appraisal from the viewpoint of the Critical Race Framework study. This only goes through two primary decision points because most racial health studies are stuck at this stage, as Martinez and colleagues' systematic review concluded regarding the lack of consistent operationalization and instrumentation (Martinez et al., 2022). 

This flowchart addresses studies that contain race as a primary variable of interest, such as conclusions about racial differences where race is an independent variable.

Does the race variable exhibit acceptable evidence of reliability? An example of a reliability measure for race might be established with test-retest reliability. It could also involve assessing changes in self-identified race with the addition of "other," enabling multiple selection, or additional categories.

-------> If acceptable, then move to validity (#2).

-------> If unacceptable, then terminate critical appraisal and data interpretation.

Does the race variable exhibit acceptable evidence for validity? Validity in the area of race should be evidence-based and meaningful.

-------> If acceptable, then move to internal validity (#2).

-------> If unacceptable, then terminate critical appraisal and data interpretation.

Terminating Critical Appraisal - Most studies are likely to terminate critical appraisal prior to evaluating internal validity. There are several options for readers, which can include asking the study authors for clarification, emailing the journal with concerns, or using for instruction.
